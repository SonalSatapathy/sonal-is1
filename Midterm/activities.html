Things to do

Visit Osaka Castle

Osaka Castle is a famous landmark in Osaka, Japan, originally built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in 1583 as a symbol of power. The castle complex includes defensive walls, moats, and gates, along with a modern, multi-story main keep that serves as a history museum. Today, the castle is a popular tourist destination surrounded by a large park, known for its historical artifacts, city views from the observation deck, and seasonal beauty, especially during cherry blossom season.

Explore the Dotonbori district

Dotonbori is a vibrant, neon-lit entertainment district in Osaka, Japan, famous for its nightlife, shopping, and diverse street food, such as takoyaki and okonomiyaki. Originally developed as a commercial and entertainment hub in the 17th century, it is situated along the Dotonbori canal and features iconic landmarks like the Glico Running Man sign. Today, visitors come to experience its energetic atmosphere, which includes countless restaurants, clubs, and shopping options.

Universal Studios Japan

Universal Studios Japan is a popular theme park located in Osaka, Japan, that opened in 2001. It was the first Universal park to open outside the United States and features rides, shows, and attractions based on famous movies and popular culture, including The Wizarding World of Harry Potter and Super Nintendo World.
