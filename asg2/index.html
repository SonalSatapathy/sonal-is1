Questions

Q1: What is the difference between structured content and semantic HTML?

The difference between a structured content and semantic HTML is a structured content is content that is "planned, developed, and connected outside an interface so that it’s ready for any interface.” A semantic HTML is markup that communicates information about meaningful relationships between document elements, as opposed to simply describing how they should look on screen.

Q2: How do they work together?

They work together by providing meaning and organization to web content, benefiting both human users and machines.

Q3: Why is it important to have structured content and semantic HTML in your web pages?

It is important to have structured content and semantic HTML in your web pages because without structure and meaning, assistive technologies can't parse your content
